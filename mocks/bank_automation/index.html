What changed, and why

The product is a personal-finance automation tool — Plaid-connected accounts, savings "buckets" with goals, and rule-based automations that fire on direct deposits, schedules, balance thresholds, round-ups, and transaction matches.

Diagnosis of the current UI. The existing screens are functional but generic: the dashboard is a wall of cards with no narrative, money flow (income → allocation → goals) is split across three pages and never visualized together, automation rules feel form-heavy rather than visual, and bucket goals show raw numbers but no on-track / behind / ETA framing. The aesthetic reads as "stock SaaS template," not "trusted financial companion."

Redesign moves:

  • Narrative dashboard. The hero answers "where is my money, and where is it going?" via a net-cash sparkline + a Sankey-like "your last paycheck, automated" flow diagram showing income splitting into buckets.
  • Goals with status, not just totals. Every bucket card shows a color stripe, target, ETA, on-track / behind chip, and the rule funding it.
  • Visual "When → Then" automations. Each rule renders as a two-card diagram (trigger chip → action chips) instead of a dense form readout, plus per-rule stats (fired count, lifetime moved).
  • One money calendar. Paychecks, bills, subscriptions, and rule firings on one timeline + a side panel projecting the month's free cash.
  • Inline allocation in transactions. A purchase shows the round-up that went to Vacation; a paycheck shows the three rules it triggered. Money is contextual, not abstract.
  • Warmer, calmer aesthetic. Off-white background, warm neutrals, deep emerald accent (money / growth / trust), earth-tone palette for buckets so each goal has a stable identity. Generous whitespace, confident tabular numerals, Inter throughout.
  • Three-step wizard for new rules. Trigger → Action → Review, with a live preview that reads as a sentence ("When a direct deposit ≥ $1,000 lands in Chase Checking, then…").
